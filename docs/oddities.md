# Oddities of the Animal Kingdom

> A field guide to the strangest phyla in the portal.
> Tardigrada (water bears) may be famous, but these are even weirder.

---

## Micrognathozoa — The One-Species Phylum

**Limnognathia maerski** — discovered in 1994 in a cold spring on Disko Island, Greenland. The only known species in its phylum. It has the most complex jaw apparatus of any animal its size (0.1 mm), with 15 separate skeletal elements operated by 14 muscles. They can also reproduce parthenogenetically. We know essentially nothing about its ecology.

---

## Cycliophora — The Lobster-Lip Hitchhiker

**Symbion pandora** / **S. americanus** — two species, both living exclusively on the mouthparts of lobsters. Discovered in 1995 by Reinhardt Kristensen (same zoologist who found Micrognathozoa and Loricifera). Their life cycle defies belief:
- Asexual feeding stage → produces a bud that becomes...
- A tiny male or a larva that seeks a new host → settles and grows into...
- A female that produces a single giant egg → the cycle repeats
- Males have no digestive system, exist only to mate, then die

They were so strange they got their own phylum. Their closest relatives are probably bryozoans.

---

## Loricifera — The Anaerobic Survivors

Microscopic (< 0.5 mm), living in marine sediment. They look like tiny armoured ghosts — their body is covered in a protective shell (lorica) with retractable head and spines. First discovered in 1983.

**The weirdest thing:** Three species (*Spinoloricus cinziae*, *Rugiloricus* sp., *Pliciloricus* sp.) were found living in the **permanently anoxic** L'Atalante basin in the Mediterranean — a deep-sea brine pool with zero oxygen and toxic hydrogen sulphide. They are the only known multi-cellular animals that live their entire lives without oxygen. Their mitochondria have evolved into hydrogenosome-like organelles.

## Priapulida — Penis Worms

Exactly what they sound like. These phallic burrowing worms live in cold marine mud, using an eversible pharynx covered in spines to swallow prey whole. They are living fossils — nearly identical forms exist in the Burgess Shale (500 mya). Some species reach 10 cm. The name comes from Priapus, the Greek god of fertility.

---

## Nematomorpha — The Mind Controllers

Horsehair worms (also called Gordian worms) are parasites that drive their insect hosts to suicide. Their larvae develop inside crickets or grasshoppers, then produce proteins that hijack the host's central nervous system, forcing it to seek water and jump in. The adult worm bursts out of the drowned host and swims away to mate. They grow up to 1 metre long but are only 1-3 mm wide, making them look like a single hair.

---

## Chaetognatha — The Planktonic Assassins

Arrow worms are transparent predators that dominate the zooplankton. They have:
- Grasping spines made of **chitin** around their mouth
- A **venom gland** to paralyse prey
- A completely **different body plan** from any other phylum (tripartite coelom, but no circulatory or respiratory system)
- They are the only major animal phylum with **no fossil record** — their soft bodies left no trace

They can detect prey from a distance using vibration-sensitive bristles (the "chaetae" that give them their name) and strike in milliseconds.

---

## Onychophora — Velvet Worms: The Missing Link

They look like caterpillars with legs, but they are not arthropods. Onychophorans are the closest living relatives of arthropods, sharing a common ancestor that lived ~540 mya. They have:
- A **walking system** that's part hydrostatic, part muscular — unlike anything else
- **Jet-powered slime glands** — they spray a protein glue that solidifies on contact, entangling prey from 30 cm away
- A **brain structure** that is half annelid, half arthropod
- No jointed legs (unlike arthropods) but velvet-soft body with nephridia (unlike annelids)
- They **give birth to live young** — rare among invertebrates

## Xenacoelomorpha — The Anusless Wonders

Microscopic marine worms with no anus, no coelom, and no through-gut. They have a simple mouth opening that leads to a digestive syncytium (a multinucleate mass). For decades they were classified as flatworms, but genomic studies revealed they might be the **earliest-branching bilaterian animals** — meaning they represent the ancestral state of all animals with bilateral symmetry. Their nervous system is a simple net, not a centralized brain.

---

## Gastrotricha — Hairybacks

Microscopic (< 0.5 mm) animals that look like tiny armoured hippos. They have:
- A **completely separate evolution** of the adhesive tubes found in bryozoans (convergent evolution)
- **Eutely** — every adult has exactly the same number of cells (around 1000)
- They can switch from sexual to parthenogenetic reproduction within a single generation
- Some species can **completely dissolve and rebuild** their own pharynx multiple times during their life

---

## Kinorhyncha — Mud Dragons

Microscopic marine animals with **11 or 13 segments** (always odd), each covered by a cuticular plate. Their head is a retractable, spiny ball of horror that telescopes out to burrow through sediment. They have no cilia, no larval stage, and a completely unique arrangement of their nervous system. Every species has exactly 9 outer oral styles around the mouth. No exceptions.

---

## Brachiopoda — Lamp Shells

They look like clams but are not even close. Brachiopods have:
- Two shells, but the **plane of symmetry goes through the shells** (not between them like bivalves)
- A **lophophore** — a spiral feeding organ that is unique to brachiopods, bryozoans, and phoronids
- They were the **dominant shelled animals of the Palaeozoic**, with thousands of fossil species. Today only ~100 species survive — relics of a bygone era.
- Their blood contains **hemerythrin** (purple, iron-based) not hemoglobin

---

## Phoronida — Horseshoe Worms

Tube-dwelling marine worms with a U-shaped gut (mouth and anus both at the same end — inside the tube). They have that same lophophore as brachiopods and bryozoans. A single phoronid can produce **thousands of clones** by budding. They are so conservative in evolution that living species are nearly indistinguishable from their Cambrian ancestors.

---

## Gnathostomulida — Jaw Worms

Microscopic worms (< 1 mm) that live exclusively in oxygen-poor marine sediment (sulfidic mud). They have:
- A **cuticular jaw apparatus** with paired basal plates, a jugum, and tiny teeth — the parts are so complex they are used for species identification
- **No anus** (like flatworms and xenacoelomorphs)
- No circulatory or respiratory system
- They feed by scraping bacteria off sand grains with their jaws
- Discovered in 1956, they were the last major animal phylum to be described

---

## Hemichordata — Acorn Worms

The weird cousins of chordates. They have:
- A **three-part body**: proboscis, collar, trunk
- **Gill slits** like fish (pharyngeal slits) — but they are used for filter feeding, not breathing
- A **stomochord** — a structure that looks like a notochord (the chordate backbone precursor) but is probably a diverticulum of the gut, not homologous
- Some produce **brominated compounds** so toxic they can kill fish
- The acorn worm *Balanoglossus* smells like **iodoform** and can grow to 2 m

---

## Entoprocta — Goblet Worms

Tiny (< 0.5 mm) sessile animals that live in gelatinous or chitinous cups (calices). Their **anus opens inside the tentacle crown** — a distinction from bryozoans (where the anus opens outside). They have a **completely different larval development** than any similar-looking animal. Some species can survive complete desiccation and revive years later when rehydrated.

## The Top 10 Weirdest Facts from the Micro-Phyla

1. **No oxygen? No problem.** Loriciferans live in hydrogen sulphide brine pools.
2. **Mind control.** Nematomorphs force crickets to drown themselves.
3. **Jaws of steel.** Micrognathozoans have the most complex microscopic jaws known.
4. **Sex on the lips.** Cycliophorans spend their lives on lobster mouthparts.
5. **You have no anus.** Xenacoelomorphs and gnathostomulids get by just fine without one.
6. **Same number of cells, forever.** Gastrotrichs have fixed cell counts (eutely).
7. **11 segments, always odd.** Kinorhyncha never have an even number.
8. **Glue gun.** Onychophorans immobilize prey with solidified protein slime.
9. **Purple blood.** Brachiopods use hemerythrin, not hemoglobin.
10. **500 million years unchanged.** Priapulids look the same as their Cambrian fossils.

# Untouched Animal Phyla Report

This report documents the major taxonomic phyla within the Animal Kingdom (*Animalia*) that are currently missing from the Systema Naturae portal. These phyla represent significant opportunities for future database expansion, curation, and enrichment.

*Report compiled on: June 26, 2026*

---

## Current Phylum Representation

The portal currently represents **5 animal phyla** out of approximately 35 known to science:
1. **Chordata** (Vertebrates and relatives)
2. **Arthropoda** (Insects, spiders, crustaceans)
3. **Mollusca** (Cephalopods, snails, bivalves)
4. **Echinodermata** (Starfish, sea urchins, sea cucumbers)
5. **Tardigrada** (Water bears)

This leaves **~30 phyla completely untouched**.

## Top Recommendations for the Next Expansion

These three phyla present the most scientifically exciting and visually rich targets for the portal's next phase of development:

### 1. Phylum **Cnidaria** (Jellyfish & Corals) — *~11,000 species*
* **Visual Appeal:** Extremely high. Perfect for generating stunning UI theme palettes (neon blues, coral pinks, translucent bio-luminescents).
* **Major Classes:**
  * *Anthozoa* (Stony corals, soft corals, sea anemones)
  * *Scyphozoa* (True jellyfish)
  * *Hydrozoa* (Siphonophores like the Portuguese man o' war, Hydra)
  * *Cubozoa* (Box jellyfish)

### 2. Phylum **Annelida** (Segmented Worms) — *~22,000 species*
* **Ecological Importance:** Extremely high. Essential soil aerators, nutrient cyclers, and benthic marine foundation species.
* **Major Classes / Groups:**
  * *Polychaeta* (Deep-sea hydrothermal tube worms, Christmas tree worms, bristle worms)
  * *Clitellata* (Earthworms and leeches)

### 3. Phylum **Ctenophora** (Comb Jellies) — *~150 species*
* **Full Curation Candidate:** Because of its small species count, this is a prime candidate for a **$100\%$ complete and enriched** branch. We could easily scrape, import, and Wikipedia-enrich every single species in the phylum, representing a perfect "Fully Curated" milestone.
